Avoiding BADDR Tool

What is BADDR?

BADDR stands for Blaming, Aggregate, Decontextualized, Deficit-based, and Restricted language. These types of language can perpetuate harmful stereotypes, oversimplify complex issues, and contribute to inequities in health outcomes. By avoiding BADDR language, we can create more respectful, accurate, and culturally sensitive narratives about Māori women's health.

Legal Requirement

Under the Health (Cervical Screening (Kaitiaki)) Regulations 1995, you are legally required to seek approval from the National Kaitiaki Group (NKG) for any disclosure, use, or publication of Māori women's cervical screening data. The NKG ensures that the data is not used or published inappropriately, does not reflect negatively on Māori, and ultimately benefits Māori women.

For any queries or support regarding cervical screening data, please contact the National Cervical Screening Programme (NCSP):

Email: ncsp@tewhatuora.govt.nz

Important Notice on Data Usage and Partnerships

The National Kaitiaki Group (NKG) plays a crucial role in protecting Māori women's cervical screening data. They ensure that such data is not used or published inappropriately or in a way that reflects negatively on Māori, and that it benefits Māori women.

Furthermore, partnerships with Māori and Pacific advisors are essential when handling Māori and Pacific data. These partnerships help ensure cultural sensitivity and appropriate use of data. For support regarding Māori and Pacific data, please reach out to the Intelligence Advisory team.
